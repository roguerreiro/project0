Taylor Swift Discography Awards Review

A brief review of some Taylor Swift songs

A brief review

Here are the most underrated songs on each Taylor Swift album and what makes them so special!

Taylor Swift - "Mary's Song (Oh My My My)"

"Mary's Song (Oh My My My)" is the 10th track off of Taylor Swift's self-titled debut album.

It tells the story of two childhood friends who grow up together and end up falling in love and getting married.

The chorus' lyrics change after each cycle, adapting to the children's growth and time passing.

The production starts with a soft acoustic guitar line, and gets progressively more intense as the song goes on.

Notable lyrics: "I'll be 87, you'll be 89 / I'll still look at you like the stars that shine / in the sky, oh my my my"

Fearless - "Breathe" (feat. Colbie Caillat)

"Breathe" is the 7th track off of Fearless, Swift's most critically aclaimed Country album.

This track covers the aftermath of a breakup gone wrong, after which both parts suffered deeply.

It goes over the feeling of having to live normally after going through such intense emotions, knowing that their love will never respark.

Swift's great vocals are showcased in the chorus, and merge beautifully with Colbie Caillat's voice throughout the song.

Notable lyrics: "People are people and sometimes we change our minds / But it's killing me to see you go after all this time"

Speak Now - "Dear John"

"Dear John" is Speak Now's 5th track, which is, by default, the most lyrically vulnerable track in every Taylor Swift Album.

This song, which also revolves around a breakup, is supposedly about Swift's relationship with musician John Mayer.

This time, the lyrics talk about the feeling of being "played" inside a relationship that was never good for her.

Throughout the song, Swift begins getting over what happened, seeing more clearly that what happened wasn't her fault.

The soft guitar in the background, combined with Swift's overwhelming vocals, make this song stand out not just lyrically, but sonically as well.

Notable lyrics: "But I took your matches / Before fire could catch me / So don't look now / I'm shining like fireworks / Over your sad, empty town"

Red - "Begin Again"

"Begin Again" is Red's closing track.

It focuses on finding love again after having a rough time in a past relationship.

The song compares how she felt in this past relationship and her newlyfound one, emphasizing how this new love is better for her

The minimalistic production of the song gives an airy feeling that passes on the emotions of the track really well.

Notable lyrics: " I've been spending the last eight months / Thinking all love ever does is break and burn and end / But on a Wednesday in a cafe / I watched it begin again"

1989 - "New Romantics"

"New Romantics" is one of the bonus tracks on 1989's deluxe version.

The song focuses not on a single relationship, but on contemporary love as a whole.

It explores the mix of emotions that is living and loving nowadays, and how your expectations and exposure can change how it goes.

Its upbeat production matches the song's optimistic lyrics and create a great mood to close the album.

Notable lyrics: "'Cause baby I could build a castle / Out of all the bricks they threw at me / And every day is like a battle / But every night with us is like a dream"

reputation - "Call It What You Want"

"Call It What You Want" is reputation's fourteenth and second-to-last track.

It has a calmer vibe compared to most of the other tracks' intense and industrial sound.

This song focuses on Swift's current relationship with actor Joe Alwyn at its beginning.

It addresses how she's been called a "serial dater" for several years, but doesn't mind anymore because of the happiness she has found in her relationship.

Notable lyrics: "I want to wear his initial on a chain 'round my neck / Chain 'round my neck / Not because he owns me / But 'cause he really knows me / Which is more than they can say, ah"

Lover - "Cornelia Street"

"Cornelia Street" is the 9th track off of Swift's most recent studio album, Lover.

Its main focus is on the memories that are permanently attached to a relationship, and how breaking up would mess with them.

Its title is a reference to an apartment that Swift rented for a few months in New York, at the time she met her current lover, Joe Alwyn.

The song's background includes a very catchy soft beat, as the lyrics go over gaining stability in this relationship that she doesn't want to lose.

Notable lyrics: "Barefoot in the kitchen / Sacred new beginnings / That became my religion, listen"
